# Digital-Literacy-project-
As a Student Digital Ambassador, I will create a Digital Literacy Portfolio to help peers understand responsible digital practices. The project will provide instructions for creating professional online profiles, demonstrating digital tool usage, teaching internet safety practices, and showing proper online communication and digital etiquette.

## Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
In this task, a one-page infographic was created using Canva to describe digital literacy and its significance to students. The infographic focused on three key topics, including digital literacy, digital tools for students, and safe internet usage. The idea behind this task was to effectively communicate key information to students in a simple and attractive way to help them realize the importance and role of digital literacy in academic and online activities. The final infographic was exported and added to the project repository.

## Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio
In this task, professional profiles were created on various online platforms that are essential in the academic and professional world. The profiles were created on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. GitHub is used for coding projects and other technical activities, LinkedIn is used for professional networking and career development, and Kaggle is used for learning and improving skills in data science and data analysis. Screenshots of the created profiles were added to the repository.

## Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms
In this task's coding practice section, an account was created on HackerRank, and a beginner programming problem was completed to assess how coding practice sites function. These sites can be beneficial to students to enhance their logical and programming skills.
In the collaboration section, a Google Form titled "Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz" was created, and five questions were added to test students' knowledge about digital literacy concepts. The answers will be stored automatically in a Google Sheet associated with this form, showing how online collaboration tools can be beneficial in gathering information.

## Task 4 – Professional Email & Digital Etiquette
In this task, professional email writing skills were practiced. Two professional emails were created: one to ask for an extension on an assignment from a professor and another to express interest in a summer internship program. Both emails demonstrate professional email etiquette, including a subject line, greeting, body, and closing. Additionally, a checklist titled "Social Media Do's and Don'ts" was created to help students learn about online etiquette and how to maintain a positive online image.

## Task 5 – Cyber Crime Awareness Case Study
The final task involved researching a cybercrime scenario and creating awareness about the threats associated with it. A case study on the scenario of phishing attacks was written, explaining what phishing is, how the perpetrators try to steal users’ information, the kind of users are targeted, and what the consequences are if users are not cautious. A Stay Safe Online checklist was also prepared, giving students advice on how to protect themselves from cyber threats like phishing, what kind of password habits are safe, and what precautions should be taken if the user has to make any online payments using UPI.
